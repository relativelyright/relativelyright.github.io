Stance

All writings that are related to the practice are based on the following stance on Buddha-Dhamma:

  1. Since what is allegedly the Dhamma exists in written form, the oldest sources1 are considered the most accurate, the least distorted, and least biased by later generations.
  2. A human found the Dhamma; saw it clearly, without delusion. This person, referred to as the Buddha, then formulated the Dhamma into words, spoke these words, which led to (1).
  3. Based on (2): There is no theological aspect to Dhamma.
  4. Based on (1), (2) and (3): The Dhamma is pragmatically verifiable by the results of human action.
  5. Based on (4): The Dhamma is taken and approached as a hypothesis.

  1. The Pali Canon (⟶ Wikipedia) ↩
