The Four Noble Truths

Ariya-sacca

Lists ←

The Four Noble Truths were formulated as four categories, each with three parts:

  • the truth itself
  • the task to apply to the truth
  • the fullfilment of the task

Truths

# Pali Translation
1 dukkha (there is) suffering; stress; unsatisfactoriness; discontent — in short, The Five Clinging-Aggregates
2   (there is) the cause of dukkha
3   (there is) the cessation of dukkha
4   (there is) the path leading to the cessation of dukkha, namely The Noble Eightfold Path

Tasks Associated With Respective Truth

Truth # Translation
1 comprehend, i.e. to comprehend dukkha
2 abandon, i.e. to abandon the cause of dukkha
3 realize the cessation, i.e. to realize the cessation of dukkha
4 develop, i.e. to develop The Noble Eightfold Path

The Fullfilment of Each Task

Task # Translation
1 comprehend to completion
2 abandon to completion
3 realize to completion
4 develop to completion

The Four Noble Truths as One Matrix

Following table of twelve permutations is called a Wheel of Dhamma.

Truth Task Fulfillment
(there is) dukkha comprehend Yes
(there is) the cause of dukkha abandon the cause Yes
(there is) the cessation of dukkha realize the cessation Yes
(there is) the path leading to the cessation of dukkha develop the path Yes
