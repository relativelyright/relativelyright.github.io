Three Types of Understanding

Lists ←

Pali Translation
sutamaya-pañña understanding that comes from studying and listening
cintamaya-pañña understanding that comes from thinking and ideas
bhavanamaya-pañña understanding that comes from developing the mind in concentration

Please note: The words understanding, knowledge and dicernment are used interchangably here. I am not sure of thier subtle differences with regard to their Pali counterpart or the context they are used.

Dīgha Nikāya 33: Three types of discernment:

discernment that comes from listening (sutamaya-pañña)
discernment that comes from thinking (cintamaya-pañña)
discernment that comes from developing (bhavanamaya-pañña)

In the words of Ajahn Lee

Extracts from Path to Peace, The Third Heading: Discernment:

Sutamaya-pañña is the discernment that comes from having listened a great deal … paying attention, taking notes, asking questions …

Cintamaya-pañña refers to thinking and evaluating so as to learn the meaning and truth of one’s beginning education. This level of education draws out the meaning of the knowledge we have gained through studying. …

Bhavanamaya-pañña: discernment that arises exclusively from training the mind in concentration. … When you engage in this form of practice, focus exclusively on the present, taking note of a single thing, not getting involved with past or future. … It arises on its own, not from cogitating along the lines of old concepts we’ve learned, but from abandoning them. Old concepts are what obscure the new knowledge that’s ready to arise.

Extract from The Skill of Release:

The understanding you gain from listening and reading (sutamaya-pañña) is like a person who has woken up but hasn’t yet opened his eyes. He doesn’t see any light, and so has to grope around uncertain, sometimes laying hold of the right things and sometimes laying hold of the wrong.

The understanding you gain from thinking (cintamaya-pañña) is like a person who has woken up but hasn’t yet left the mosquito net and hasn’t yet washed the sleep out of his eyes. His vision is blurry and unclear.

As for the understanding you gain from meditation (bhavanamaya-pañña), that’s like getting out of the mosquito net and washing your face so that you’re able to see things clearly. This is the highest kind of understanding. Try to develop it.

Please carefully consider everything mentioned here for yourself.
